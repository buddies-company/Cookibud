## 1. Introduction and Brand Identity

|Element|Description|
|-------|-----------|
|**Brand Name**|Cookibud|
|**Concept**|Weekly meal planning application. The name is a blend of "Cooki" (cooking) and "Bud" (friend/pal), conveying simplicity, help, and friendliness.|
|**Mission**|To simplify the organization of weekly meals in a fun and intuitive way.|
|**Tone of Voice**|Friendly, Warm, Encouraging, Simple.|

## 2. The Logotype

The logo consists of the Mascot (the little chef) and the Text Logotype (Cookibud).

### 2.1. Variations and Usage

|Variation|Description|Primary Use|
|---------|-----------|-----------|
|**Primary**|Mascot + Text + Tagline.|General communications, social media, homepage.|
|**Horizontal**|Mascot on the left, "Cookibud" text on the right.|Website headers on desktop, banners, large format printing.|
|**Vertical (Stacked)**|Mascot on top, "Cookibud" text below.|Mobile website headers, clothing labels, business cards.|
|**Icon / Favicon**|Mascot's Head (with chef hat) or the fork and knife icon.|Website URL (Favicon), app icon, avatar, and profile picture.|
|**Text-only Logo**|The word "Cookibud" alone.|Website listings, advertising materials (when context is clear), merchandise.|

### 2.2. Clear Space and Dimensions

- **Clear Space**: Always leave an empty space around the logo equal to the height of the letter "C" in the logotype to ensure its visibility and integrity.
- **Minimum Size**: Ensure that the mascot's height is never less than 50px (for the icon) or that the text remains readable at the minimum required size (recommended 15mm width for printing).

## 4. Typography

The typography must be **sans-serif**, **rounded**, and **modern** to reinforce the friendly nature of the brand.

### 4.1. Recommended Fonts

| Role | Recommended Font | Weight | Usage |
| ---- | ---------------- | ------ | ----- |
| **Primary Title (H1)** | **Poppins** (or Comfortaa) | Bold to ExtraBold | Section titles, headlines, large text. |
| **Secondary Title (H2/H3)** | **Montserrat** | Semi-Bold | Subheadings, card titles. |
| **Body Text (P)** | **Montserrat** (or Roboto) | Regular | Recipe descriptions, blog articles, body content.|

### 4.2. Usage Rules

- **Hierarchy**: Use the `#643A2C` color for titles in Light Mode.
- **Size**: Titles must be significantly larger than the body text. A modular size scale is encouraged (e.g., 1.25x the previous element).

## 5. Complementary Graphic Elements

### 5.1. Iconography

- **Style**: **Outline** or **Flat** icons. Shapes must be **rounded** and **geometric** to match the logo style.
- **Color**: Primarily use the **Warm Brown (`#643A2C`)** or the **Accent color (`#E08535`)** for interactive icons.
- **Examples**: To represent a calendar, use a simple line drawing with the days, not a complex illustration.

### 5.2. Illustration and Photography

- **Illustration Style**: Must align with the Cookibud Mascot: **Cartoon style, cute, with distinct outlines and bright colors from the primary palette.**
- **Photography**: If photos are used (e.g., recipe photos), they must be high-quality, well-lit, and emphasize the **warmth** and **freshness** of the ingredients. Avoid overly dark or cold photos.

### 5.3. Technical and Export Data

- **File Formats (Logo)**:
    - **SVG (Scalable Vector Graphics)**: Required for web and very large format printing (for loss-less scaling).
    - **PNG (Portable Network Graphics)**: For web, mobile applications, and documents requiring a transparent background.
- **Color Space**:
    - **RGB / HEX**: For all digital uses (web, mobile, screens).
    - **CMYK**: For professional printing (invoices, labels).
- **Resolution**: All bitmap exports (PNG, JPG) intended for the web must be optimized and, if possible, in 2x (retina) for icons and logos.
